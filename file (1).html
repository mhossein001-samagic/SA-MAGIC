Project Spotlight

“Clean Lane” — Waste-to-Beauty Transformation

A 3-week neighborhood effort combining cleanup, segregation awareness, and beautification through murals and plants.

  • Installed segregation signboards and coordinated weekly pickup reminders
  • Cleared illegal dumping points and created a monitored “no-dump” zone
  • Ran a door-to-door civic complaint support drive
Sponsor the next lane Volunteer with the team

Civic Engagement Education Hub

Simple, practical resources to help citizens participate—without jargon.

How to file a civic complaint (step-by-step)

Identify the right department, write a clear description, attach photos, note location details, and follow up with a reference number.

Ward meeting checklist: what to ask and how to follow up

Prepare 2–3 issues, bring evidence, ask for timelines, record commitments, and share outcomes with residents.

RTI basics (beginner-friendly)

Use RTI to request official information like tender details, project timelines, and spending records—legally and respectfully.

Voter participation: “Before you vote” guide

Verify your registration, understand local issues, compare promises with evidence, and encourage informed participation.
